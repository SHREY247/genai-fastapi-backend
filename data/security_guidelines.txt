ACME CORP — INFORMATION SECURITY GUIDELINES
=============================================

VERSION: 3.1 | EFFECTIVE DATE: March 1, 2025

PURPOSE
--------
These guidelines establish the information security standards that all Acme Corp
employees, contractors, and third-party users must follow. The objective is to
protect company data, customer information, and IT infrastructure from
unauthorized access, misuse, and cyber threats.

SECTION 1: PASSWORD POLICY
----------------------------
1. Passwords must be at least 14 characters long.
2. Passwords must include at least one uppercase letter, one lowercase letter,
   one number, and one special character.
3. Passwords must be changed every 90 days.
4. The last 12 passwords cannot be reused.
5. Passwords must not contain the user's name, username, or common dictionary
   words.
6. Default passwords on any system or device must be changed immediately upon
   first login.
7. Password managers are encouraged. The company provides a free license for
   1Password to all employees.

SECTION 2: MULTI-FACTOR AUTHENTICATION (MFA)
----------------------------------------------
1. MFA is mandatory for all company applications including email, VPN, cloud
   services, and internal tools.
2. Approved MFA methods include:
   - Hardware security keys (YubiKey) — preferred
   - Authenticator apps (Google Authenticator, Microsoft Authenticator)
   - SMS-based OTP — allowed but discouraged due to SIM-swap risks
3. Employees must register at least two MFA methods as backup.
4. MFA enrollment must be completed within 24 hours of account creation.
5. Exceptions require written approval from the CISO.

SECTION 3: DEVICE SECURITY
----------------------------
1. All company-issued laptops must have full-disk encryption enabled (BitLocker
   for Windows, FileVault for macOS).
2. Automatic screen lock must be configured to activate after 5 minutes of
   inactivity.
3. Operating system and software updates must be installed within 72 hours of
   release.
4. Antivirus software must be installed and kept up to date on all endpoints.
5. USB ports are disabled by default on company devices. Exceptions require IT
   Security approval.
6. Personal devices used for work (BYOD) must:
   - Be registered with the IT department
   - Have a mobile device management (MDM) agent installed
   - Meet minimum OS version requirements
   - Be remotely wipeable in case of loss or theft

SECTION 4: NETWORK AND ACCESS CONTROL
---------------------------------------
1. Access to internal systems follows the principle of least privilege. Users
   are granted only the permissions necessary for their role.
2. Access reviews are conducted quarterly. Managers must certify that their
   team members' access is still appropriate.
3. VPN must be used when accessing company resources from outside the office
   network.
4. The company network is segmented into zones:
   - Corporate zone: general employee access
   - Development zone: restricted to engineering teams
   - Production zone: restricted to authorized DevOps personnel
   - Guest zone: isolated network for visitors, no access to internal resources
5. All network traffic is monitored and logged for security analysis.
6. Open Wi-Fi networks must not be used for any work-related activities without
   an active VPN connection.

SECTION 5: EMAIL AND COMMUNICATION SECURITY
---------------------------------------------
1. Employees must not open attachments or click links from unknown or suspicious
   senders.
2. Phishing simulation exercises are conducted monthly. Employees who fail two
   consecutive simulations must complete additional security training.
3. Sensitive data must not be sent via email without encryption. Use the
   company-approved secure email gateway for classified communications.
4. Auto-forwarding of company email to personal accounts is prohibited.
5. All email is scanned for malware and data loss prevention (DLP) policy
   violations.

SECTION 6: DATA CLASSIFICATION AND HANDLING
---------------------------------------------
Data is classified into four categories:
1. Public — information intended for public release (e.g., marketing materials)
2. Internal — general business information not intended for external sharing
3. Confidential — sensitive information such as financial data, employee records
4. Restricted — highly sensitive data such as customer PII, trade secrets,
   and source code

Handling requirements by classification:
- Public: no special restrictions
- Internal: do not share externally without approval
- Confidential: encrypt in transit and at rest, limit access to authorized
  personnel
- Restricted: encrypt everywhere, access only via approved secure systems,
  log all access events, annual review required

SECTION 7: INCIDENT RESPONSE
------------------------------
1. Any suspected security incident must be reported to the Security Operations
   Center (SOC) immediately via security@acmecorp.com or phone extension 9111.
2. Do not attempt to investigate or remediate incidents independently.
3. Preserve all evidence — do not delete emails, logs, or files related to the
   incident.
4. The incident response team will:
   a. Acknowledge the report within 30 minutes
   b. Begin investigation within 1 hour
   c. Provide status updates every 4 hours during active incidents
   d. Conduct a post-incident review within 5 business days
5. Critical incidents (data breaches involving customer PII) must be escalated
   to the CISO and Legal team within 1 hour.

SECTION 8: CLOUD SECURITY
---------------------------
1. Only approved cloud services may be used for storing company data.
2. Approved services include: AWS, Azure, Google Cloud (for infrastructure),
   Google Workspace, Microsoft 365 (for productivity).
3. Shadow IT (unauthorized cloud services) is prohibited. The IT team conducts
   regular audits to identify unauthorized services.
4. Cloud storage must have:
   - Encryption at rest and in transit
   - Access controls aligned with the principle of least privilege
   - Logging and monitoring enabled
   - Regular backup and recovery testing

SECTION 9: PHYSICAL SECURITY
------------------------------
1. Access badges must be worn visibly at all times on company premises.
2. Tailgating (following someone through a secured door) is prohibited.
3. Visitors must sign in at reception and be escorted by an employee at
   all times.
4. Sensitive documents must be stored in locked cabinets when not in use.
5. Clean desk policy: all sensitive materials must be secured at the end
   of each workday.
6. Server rooms and data centers require separate key-card access limited
   to authorized IT personnel.

SECTION 10: COMPLIANCE AND TRAINING
-------------------------------------
1. All employees must complete annual security awareness training.
2. New employees must complete security training within the first week.
3. Specialized training is required for:
   - Developers: secure coding practices (OWASP Top 10)
   - Managers: access review and approval responsibilities
   - IT staff: incident response and forensics procedures
4. Compliance with these guidelines is audited annually by the internal audit
   team and periodically by external assessors.
5. Non-compliance may result in disciplinary action up to and including
   termination.
