ACME CORP — HUMAN RESOURCES POLICY HANDBOOK (2025 EDITION)
==========================================================

SECTION 1: CODE OF CONDUCT
---------------------------
All employees of Acme Corp are expected to maintain the highest standards of
professional and ethical conduct. This includes treating colleagues, customers,
and partners with respect at all times. Discrimination, harassment, and
retaliation of any kind are strictly prohibited. Violations will be
investigated promptly and may result in disciplinary action up to and including
termination of employment. Employees are encouraged to report concerns through
the anonymous ethics hotline or directly to their HR Business Partner.

SECTION 2: ATTENDANCE AND WORK HOURS
-------------------------------------
Standard working hours are 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM, Monday through Friday. Flexible
work arrangements may be approved by the employee's direct manager and HR.
Remote work is permitted up to three days per week for eligible roles as
determined by departmental policy. Any changes to the agreed schedule must be
communicated at least 48 hours in advance. Chronic absenteeism or tardiness will
be addressed through a progressive disciplinary framework beginning with verbal
counseling and escalating to written warnings and potential termination.

SECTION 3: LEAVE POLICIES
--------------------------
Employees accrue 18 days of paid time off (PTO) per calendar year, prorated for
mid-year joiners. In addition, the company observes 10 national holidays.
Sick leave is available up to 12 days per year, with a doctor's note required
for absences exceeding three consecutive days. Maternity leave is 26 weeks for
birthing parents, and paternity leave is 2 weeks. Employees may also apply for
unpaid leave of absence for personal reasons, subject to manager and HR approval.

SECTION 4: PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT
-----------------------------------
Performance reviews are conducted semi-annually. The first review takes place in
June and the second in December. Reviews consist of a self-assessment, a manager
assessment, and a peer-feedback component. Employees are rated on a 5-point scale
from "Needs Improvement" to "Exceeds Expectations." Ratings directly influence
annual bonus calculations and promotion eligibility. Goal-setting for the next
review cycle must be completed within two weeks of each review. The company uses
the OKR (Objectives and Key Results) framework for goal alignment.

SECTION 5: COMPENSATION AND BENEFITS
--------------------------------------
Salaries are benchmarked against industry standards using annual compensation
surveys. The company offers a comprehensive benefits package including medical,
dental, and vision insurance for employees and dependents. A 401(k) retirement
plan with a 4% employer match is available after 90 days of employment. Life
insurance at 2x annual salary and long-term disability coverage are provided at
no cost to the employee. Employees may also participate in the Employee Stock
Purchase Plan (ESPP) at a 15% discount.

SECTION 6: TRAVEL AND REIMBURSEMENT GUIDELINES
------------------------------------------------
Employees traveling on company business are eligible for reimbursement of
reasonable expenses. Domestic travel requires pre-approval from the employee's
manager. International travel requires additional approval from the department
VP and the finance team.

Reimbursable expenses include:
- Economy-class airfare (business class permitted for flights over 6 hours with VP approval)
- Hotel accommodations up to $200 per night domestically and $300 per night internationally
- Meals up to $75 per day domestically, $100 per day internationally
- Ground transportation including ride-sharing services
- Internet and phone charges incurred during travel

Non-reimbursable expenses include:
- Alcoholic beverages
- Personal entertainment
- Upgrades without prior approval
- Expenses incurred by non-employees (unless pre-approved client entertainment)

All reimbursement claims must be submitted within 30 days of the trip via the
internal expense management portal. Receipts are required for any single expense
exceeding $25. Late submissions may be denied at the discretion of the finance
team. Direct deposits for approved reimbursements are typically processed within
10 business days.

SECTION 7: WORKPLACE SAFETY
-----------------------------
Acme Corp is committed to maintaining a safe and healthy workplace. All
employees must complete mandatory safety training within their first 30 days.
Fire drills are conducted quarterly. Each floor has designated fire wardens
responsible for guiding evacuation. First-aid kits are located near each
pantry area. Any workplace injury, no matter how minor, must be reported to
the facilities team and HR within 24 hours. The company maintains workers'
compensation insurance for all employees.

SECTION 8: ANTI-BRIBERY AND CORRUPTION
----------------------------------------
Employees must comply with all applicable anti-bribery and corruption laws,
including the Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA) and the UK Bribery Act.
Gifts to or from clients, vendors, or government officials must not exceed $100
in value and must be disclosed via the compliance portal. Cash gifts of any
amount are strictly prohibited. Employees who become aware of potential bribery
or corruption must report immediately to the Legal and Compliance team.

SECTION 9: DATA PRIVACY
-------------------------
Personal data of employees, customers, and partners must be handled in
compliance with all applicable data protection regulations, including GDPR
and CCPA. Access to personal data must be limited to those with a legitimate
business need. Employees must complete annual data privacy training. Any
suspected data breach must be reported to the Information Security team within
one hour of discovery consistent with Section 10 of the security guidelines.

SECTION 10: GRIEVANCE AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION
----------------------------------------------
Employees who have workplace grievances may initiate the resolution process by
submitting a formal complaint to HR. The HR team will acknowledge receipt within
two business days and aim to resolve the matter within 15 business days.
Mediation services are available for disputes between employees. If the matter
remains unresolved, it may be escalated to the VP of People Operations. The
company prohibits any form of retaliation against employees who raise good-faith
complaints.

SECTION 11: TERMINATION AND OFFBOARDING
-----------------------------------------
Voluntary resignations require a minimum notice period of 30 days for individual
contributors and 60 days for managers and above. The HR team will schedule an
exit interview, and IT will coordinate the return of all company assets
including laptops, access badges, and parking permits. Final paychecks
including accrued but unused PTO will be issued within 15 days of the last
working day. Non-compete and non-solicitation obligations continue for 12 months
post-separation as outlined in the employment agreement.
